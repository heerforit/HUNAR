The Problem

A design failure,
not a
competence
failure.

India's 100 million informal workers fail every certification system — not because they lack skill, but because those systems require literacy. The credential gap is entirely a problem of design.

✗

Written tests exclude the majority

Every existing skill certification — NSDC, Skill India, Urban Company — requires reading and writing in Hindi or English.

✗

No credential means no leverage

Without proof of skill, workers cannot negotiate higher wages, access micro-loans, or find formal employment.

✗

Platform credentials lock workers in

Urban Company's certification only works inside Urban Company. Leave the platform and your credential disappears.

✗

100 million people with invisible skill

The informal workforce doing skilled work in India is larger than the entire workforce of most countries. Their expertise is real. The system to recognise it does not exist.

How It Works

Four minutes.
Any language.
One certificate.

HUNAR works entirely through voice and demonstration. The worker never reads a single word.

01
Select Language
Worker chooses Hindi, English, Rajasthani, or Bhojpuri. One tap. The entire assessment from this point is in their language.
02
Select Skill
Worker selects their profession — Cook, Tailor, Caregiver, Weaver, or Construction. The assessment loads questions specific to that skill.
03
Voice Assessment
The app reads each question aloud. The worker speaks their answer. No typing. No reading. Three questions that only a professional can answer well.
04
Certificate Issued
A QR-verified digital certificate is generated. Level 1, 2, or 3 based on responses. Share it on WhatsApp to any employer in seconds.

Business Model

Three revenue streams. Zero cost to workers.

HUNAR earns from employers and government — not from the workers it serves. The budget to solve this problem already exists.

₹500
Employer Placement Fee
Gig platforms like Urban Company pay ₹500 per HUNAR-certified worker placed. Pre-verified workers reduce complaint rates and onboarding cost. Identical to LinkedIn Recruiter's model applied to the gig economy.
B2B · Immediate ROI for buyer
₹2k
Institution Subscription
NGOs and vocational training centres pay ₹2,000/month for a HUNAR dashboard to issue and track certificates — giving them verifiable proof of impact for donors and government reports.
Recurring SaaS · Low churn
₹40
NSDC Government Scheme
PMKVY already reimburses assessment agencies ₹40–120 per person. HUNAR applies to be empanelled. The government pays for every assessment. Budget allocated: ₹12,000 crore. We are the better pipe.
Govt scheme · Zero new spending
